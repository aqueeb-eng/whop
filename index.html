0 – 200 m

Sunlight Zone

The ocean's surface layer receives enough light to support photosynthesis. Home to 90% of all marine life and the world's fisheries.

🌑
200 – 1,000 m

Twilight Zone

Only faint, filtered light penetrates here. Creatures use bioluminescence to hunt, communicate, and hide from predators lurking below.

🦑
1,000 – 4,000 m

Midnight Zone

Pitch black and bone-cold. The pressure here is 400 times that at the surface. Giant squid and sperm whales patrol these waters.

🌋
4,000 – 11,000 m

Hadal Zone

Named after Hades, the underworld. Found only in ocean trenches, yet life stubbornly thrives — microbes, snailfish, and amphipods.
